About Us

Pioneering sustainable telematics solutions for over two decades.

Introducing DOT

DOT, a financially strong Austrian family-owned company, combines IT-specialists with a manufactory of highest quality standards, as well as innovative and fully sustainable technology. This company is a pioneer in the field of digital devices in the logistics industry, with a focus on railway systems.

DOT continually impresses its customers with individual solutions for telematics and GPS tracking systems. Its patented X-Rayl system has been rolled out all across Europe, selling over 50.000 durable, solar powered and easy-to-use telematic systems.

Our Company History

2022

100% sustainable office building under construction, opening planned for 2023.

A new milestone: Over 50,000 X-Rayl Pointers sold.

Development of a low-cost King Pin Solution.

2021

Certification of the Sensor S3 for the highest ATEX, UKCA and IECEx standards.

2020

Development of Sensor S3 (based on Bluetooth Low Energy) which, as a platform is now capable of distance, temperature, pressure, attitude, vibration, acceleration Hi G and acceleration Low G.

All our S3 devices were certified ATEX und IECEx (IIC T6 Gb).

2019

Enhanced ATEX Certification for X-Rayl Pointer S2.
(II 2G Ex ib IIC T6 Gb for Gas and II 2D Ex ib IIIC T85°C Db for Dust).

2018

Joined as a member of ITSS.

Development of the Sensor Box (IEEE 802.15.4, 2,4GHz) for temperatures up to 260°C, tilt, humidity, derailment, loading status and laser ranging.

2017

A new milestone: Over 1000 X-Rayl Pointer in use.

Development of new Series 17M Pointer with almost three times more energy and better positioning precision.

2015

Extension of the ATEX Certification (ATEX 94/9/EC) of the X-Rayl Solar Pointers to Zone 1 and Zone 21.

2014

Deployment of the DOT-Link Version 4.0 including features such as Geofencing for numerous Customers and a Map-Matching System for Eye-contact Analysis for the Advertising Industry.

2012

Opening of the new Operation Site connected to a Solar Power Plant in Leobendorf, Lower Austria.

2011

The X-Rayl mileage meter ODO2 is fully developed and records wagons' travelled distances with an accuracy of 99.8%.

2005

A patent is granted on the solar-energy powered GPS positioning device “X-Rayl® Pointer”.

2004

The German railway company Deutsche Bahn (DB) places the first substantial order for X-Rayl. The quality and state of the GSM-R network are recorded in a position-dependent manner.

2003

Philipp Tarter dreams of offering innovative, ground-breaking telematics solutions but finds the market lacking flexible and functional products. He decides to found DOT-Telematik GmbH in order to be able to develop and produce solutions himself.
