Data Processing and Presentation

The telematics and sensor data collected by DOT is transmitted to and processed by a collection of software services which have been developed to be able to process billions of data points each day.

The data gets parsed, analysed, and enriched while passing through the DOT backend services and the output datasets are then stored in a high availability data store. This data can then be accessed either by low level access through the DOT Unified API, or via the web application DOT Link 4.0 which provides an intuitive user interface to explore the vast dataset and provide our customers with valuable insights into their fleet.

DOT analytics backend

The DOT analytics backend is the core through which all data passes. It provides interfaces for incoming data from X-Rayl devices, as well as for third party telematics and sensory data. This data is enriched by metadata like asset identification, geofences, mileage and much more. The final data set contains all relevant information and statistics and is available through the DOT Unified API.

DOT Unified API

The Unified API gives unmatched direct access to all telematics, sensor and administrative data and features in the DOT analytics backend, from administrative tasks like creating new assets or geofences to querying every aspect of the data. Since it is accessible through a single API endpoint our customers can easily integrate DOT tightly into their internal ERP systems.

Since the security of our customers data is of utmost importance, all functionalities of the DOT Unified API are protected by the highest authentication and encryption standards.

DOT Link 4.0

DOT Link 4.0 is the new and improved version of our Web Front End, giving our users easy and comprehensive access to their asset / fleet analytics and telematics as well as sensor data.

Track and trace of assets, administrative tasks like adding new users, assets or geofences have been streamlined to provide a more efficient and thorough way of managing operational data. The user interface is intuitive and provides our customers with an experience customised to their needs.

Data Enrichment

The key to generating valuable insights from data processed by telematic devices and sensors is data enrichment.

Through our long-term involvement in the rail and container business, we have the know-how to create real world value for our customers, through analysis and enrichment of the huge amounts of collected data. From high accuracy mileage calculation to geofencing or assessment of operational efficiency, the benefits derived from analytics save our customers time and money each day.

Data Enrichment turns raw data into useful information. Mileage is calculated from individual positions allowing for optimisation of maintenance cycles. Flat spots on wheelsets are detected through vibrations avoiding derailments. Exceeded temperature limits lead to alarms, protecting life and infrastructure. Loading conditions are detected and lead to efficient dispatching.

The right application makes digitalisation useful!

Mileage calculation

Rail based assets are susceptible to fluctuations in utilisation. Currently the rolling stock with all its components must be maintained at regular intervals, for safety reasons. Considerable savings are possible, when the exact mileage of each wagon is known, by carrying out maintenance in a targeted manner, rather than in fixed intervals.

DOT's tested and verified algorithm records mileage with an accuracy of over 98%.

Start-Stop recognition

Pointers from the S17 series upwards have an integrated high-precision acceleration sensor that detects any form of movement. With the "start-stop" feature, the transmission frequency can be reduced at standstill to save significant amounts of energy. The transmission interval reduction is set by a freely configurable factor.

The start of an assets trip (container, freight wagon, etc.) is reported within two minutes. Standstills are reported after about five minutes.

Geofencing

Geofencing generates an event or alarm when an asset (container, freight car, etc.) enters, exits, or crosses a geographical barrier or zone. The geo-zones can be freely defined, either with an editor in the DOT-Link platform or via the associated DOT Unified API endpoint. Notifications can be configured for each type of geofencing event, to instantly notify users about important events.

Idle time detection

Provides alerts in case of long idle times

Our customers are able to monitor the standing times of their assets (containers, freight wagons) and to receive automatic alarms if specific maximum times are exceeded. In combination with geofencing and load detection, this feature allows for quick notifications exactly when and where they are relevant and desired.

Brake health indicator

A significant factor in terms of wear on brake systems is the amount of scaled altitude. To better estimate this wear and consequently optimise maintenance, DOT has developed the X-Rayl DOT-Link Altimeter algorithm, which precisely calculates such altitude.

The basis for the scaled altitude is a high-resolution digital model. This approach is far superior to a purely GPS-based solution due to the data accuracy.

Wagon turnaround and times analysis

Gain ongoing analyses of wagon movements based on predefined locations and routes.

Wagon movements and standstills are the basic indicator of the transport efficiency of goods or raw materials. Rented wagons or containers usually cost money over time. Assets that wait too long for filling or emptying indicate the potential for improvement. This feature can be used to display sophisticated statistics and tables that can quickly reveal inefficiencies.

Map matching

Map matching provides additional GIS information beyond the geographical position.

Geographical coordinates are clearly defined but are not necessarily usable in this form immediately. Map matching compares the measured positions with map material and enriches the data sets with additional information such as current country or the nearest town. Thanks to Map matching, you can find out on which track or road segment your assets are located or exactly which route they have travelled.

Tank carload detection

Tank cars are often equipped with temperature sensors. If this is the case, loading and unloading can be detected by a sophisticated algorithm which compares the temperature behavior with stored empirical values and thus allows an accurate estimation of the load state. If only the load state is required, rather than the exact weighing, this feature renders more complex systems, which detect the filling level via immersion rods or similar means, obsolete.

Load monitoring

By deploying an X-Rayl Sensor S3DST on a railcar, the distance between the wagon's superstructure and the wheel set can be measured with millimeter precision. From the measured data, it is possible to determine when the wagon is being loaded or unloaded, as well as provide a rough estimate on the loading level including overload detection.

Overload detection

By capturing the data of an X-Rayl Sensor S3DST on a railcar not only the loading state (see above) can be detected, but also a potential overload, which can cause damage to the railcar.

Container / tank heating analysis

For some substances, transported by containers and railcars, the temperature must be within specified limits. The heating process to achieve this is done by the means of a pipe which guides hot water or steam through the tank-container. For the purpose of the entire heating process, a sensor box is screwed onto a specially developed device and attached directly to the pipe. A gradient recognition system monitors the heating process with great precision and records the temperature profile.

Statistics and Reports

Geostatistics

Different regulations might require the registration of the driven kilometers in a specific country i.e. how many kilometers the car has driven in Germany or Hungary, how many of them were travelled in a loaded or unloaded state. In order to achieve this, not only the route has to be precisely recorded, a mark has to be made at each border crossing to enable geo-statistics.

Asset usage rate analysis

Determine whether an asset is in use or not.

A depot is a special geofence with which the rate of use can be calculated. If an asset is located within the depot, it is considered unused, for example. If it is located outside, it is considered in use. With this feature, statistics on the usage can be easily stored for each asset.

Asset Condition Monitoring

Know the state of your asset

Advanced shock detection

The X-Rayl Pointers as well as the X-Rayl Sensor S3ACC-D include three high-precision 3-axis accelerometers featuring a wide array of detection ranges. From measuring small changes in the mg range as well as shocks up to 200g with a high resolution, all sensors can detect all shock scenarios, expected in the railway environment.

Configurable frequency filters allow precise detection of all types of shock events and adjustable thresholds allow immediate alarming when exceeded.

Derailment and theft detection

Receive early alarm in case of excessive changes of an assets orientation.

X-Rayl Pointers have acceleration sensors that detect the precise orientation of the asset they are mounted to. In the event of a derailment, the orientation of the asset changes significantly, which makes early alarming possible. Dismounting the X-Rayl Pointer from the asset also leads to an alarm allowing for theft detection.

Wagon Health Indicator

Diverse environmental conditions and a multitude of other factors, such as mileage, load state track condition, and more make ascertaining the necessity of a rail asset's maintenance challenging.

X-Rayl Pointers measure vibration patterns, detecting damage to the wheel sets, bogies or the superstructure allowing an algorithm to determine the Wagon Health Indicator, which starts at a value of 100% and gradually decreases on the detection of damage, with daily updates.

The indicator enables detecting assets in need of extra maintenance.

Brake Health Indicator

Timely maintenance of railcar brake systems is an important factor in order to keep railcars operational at maximum utilisation, and to avoid disruptions to logistical flows.

An algorithm processes the data generated by the X-Rayl Sensor S3PRS-D to analyse varying brake pressure profiles and the brake application frequency, allowing for estimation of resulting brake wear.

This Brake Health Indicator allows our customers to predictively apply maintenance to cars with high brake utilisation.

Advanced event handling

Input individual alarm thresholds of sensors.

Via the DOT-Link web portal, events (geofence, door openings, etc.) can be linked to SMS or e-mail addresses by default, which will be alerted when an event occurs. The Advanced Event Handling feature is necessary if, for example, thresholds such as temperature limits are to be set, that require a threshold value to be transmitted to a sensor. This is typically the case when monitoring a tank container's temperature sensor.

Sub 1GHz preparation

Overcome larger distances of several wagon lengths by means of "Sub 1GHz”.

The future technology for intra-train communication is not yet clearly defined. Rail associations are contemplating the use of the 2.4GHz interface, but considering the uncertainties, the LM-EJ provides a dedicated radio transmitter and receiver for sub 1Ghz with a power of 20 dBm. Although an external antenna is required, the Sub 1Ghz offers several advantages over the 2.4Ghz spectrum, such as a 1.5 - 2 times higher transmission range, lower power consumption and less interference.

Data Sharing

Providing relevant data to your customers

External Interface Package

Transfer data to third-party systems

Compatibility of telematic applications from different providers has not been guaranteed, because data exchange was not standardised, but nowadays the ITSS deals with the standardisation of interfaces. DOT is a participant in the program and provides telematics data in compliance with its standards.

In addition, the DOT Unified API offers a comprehensive interface to all data not covered by the ITSS standard, enabling close integration into existing internal processes.

Configurable Data Sharing

When renting out assets to customers it is of utmost importance that only the asset data of the agreed rental period is available to the customer. The DOT Unified API and DOT Link allow asset owners to share data with a fine granularity of permissions and automatic limitation of share time frames according to specified criteria. Since the administration of the shared data can be done entirely via the DOT Unified API, coupling it to internal contract data can be achieved with relative ease. This way no duplicate effort has to be made to manage data across internal systems and the DOT platform.

Dynamic user management

Manage user functionality via API or DOT-Link.

Whether it's creating a new user or changing a password, the toolkit provided by the DOT Unified API and DOT-Link makes managing user administration quick and simple. Your organisation's active users can be handled entirely through the provided interfaces and can be coupled to internal systems. This allows automatic deactivation of user accounts for people leaving a company or work group and provides full transparency of all active accounts and user actions.
